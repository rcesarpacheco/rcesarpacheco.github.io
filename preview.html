Welcome! I am a PhD candidate in the Department of Economics at Columbia University, expected to graduate in 2027.

My research spans macroeconomics, finance, and banking. I study how information frictions shape financial markets and firm financing decisions.

I am on the job market in 2026–2027. My job market paper studies coordination and welfare in equity financing.

Research

Working papers

Job market paper
Information, Coordination and Welfare in Equity Financing
This paper studies how information frictions and coordination among investors affect the efficiency of equity financing. A coordination distortion arises from the wedge between the profit-sharing rule and the aggregation technology, generating novel welfare implications for market design.
Paper Slides Online appendix
Bank Competition, Productivity and Inequality
This paper studies how bank competition affects firm productivity and inequality.

Household Assets and the Job Ladder
with Madeline Hanson
This paper studies job-to-job transitions and wage dynamics, building on continuous-time heterogeneous agent methods and using NLSY data.
